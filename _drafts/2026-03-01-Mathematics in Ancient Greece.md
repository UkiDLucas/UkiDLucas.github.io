Mathematics in Ancient Greece had a profound influence on the world, contributing foundational concepts, theorems, and philosophical principles that are still taught and used today.

Here are some of the significant contributions:

1.  **[[Pythagoras]] and [[Pythagoreanism]]**: Pythagoras was a [[philosopher]] and [[mathematician]] who founded a school of thought known as Pythagoreanism. The most famous theorem attributed to him is the [[Pythagorean theorem]], which states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.
    
2.  **[[Euclidean]] [[geometry 1]]**: Euclid's "[[Elements]]" is one of the most influential works in the history of mathematics, serving as the main textbook for teaching mathematics (especially geometry) from its publication until the late 19th or early 20th century. It includes the [definitions], [[postulates (axioms)]], [[theorems]], and [[mathematical proofs]] of the geometric concepts we use today.
    
3.  **[[number theory 1]]**: The Greeks made significant contributions to the early development of number theory. [[Diophantus]], often known as the '[[father of algebra]],' wrote a series of books called "[[Arithmetica]]," which were the first to take an algebraic approach to solving equations.
    
4.  **[[Solid Geometry]]**: [[Archimedes]], one of the greatest mathematicians of the [[ancient world]], made significant contributions to solid geometry, among other fields. His methods anticipated many of the [[integral calculus]] techniques developed much later.
    
5.  **[[Mathematical Logic]]**: The systematic treatment of logic by the Greeks was a precursor to the formal systems used in modern mathematical logic.
    
6.  **[[Mathematical Notation (in LaTeX) 1]]**: The Greeks developed mathematical notation that, while not identical to the one we use today, was a precursor to modern mathematical notation.
    
7.  **Mathematical Philosophy**: Greek mathematicians were the first to establish mathematics as a [[deductive science]]. They insisted on [[rigorous logic]] and [[proofs in mathematics]], setting the stage for the way mathematics is practiced today.
    
8.  **[[Trigonometry 1]]**: Though trigonometry was more fully developed by the [[Indians]], the Greeks made important early contributions. [[Hipparchus]], for example, is known as the founder of trigonometry, a branch of mathematics that deals with the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles.
    

These contributions, among others, made ancient Greece a cornerstone of Western mathematical thought and tradition. Greek mathematics shaped the discipline in ways that are still evident today.
