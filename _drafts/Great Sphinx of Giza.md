The theory of water erosion on the Sphinx was proposed by John Anthony West, an independent Egyptologist, and was further studied by Dr. Robert M. Schoch, a geologist and associate professor of Natural Sciences at the College of General Studies at Boston University. According to their hypothesis, the erosional features on the Sphinx and the surrounding enclosure walls could only have been caused by long-term rainfall, which is believed to have last occurred in the region around 5000–7000 BCE, during the Neolithic Subpluvial period.

However, this theory is highly controversial and is not accepted by mainstream Egyptologists, who maintain that the Sphinx was carved around 2500 BCE during the reign of the Pharaoh Khafre. The orthodox view suggests that the erosion is due to wind and sand, not water.

Critics of the water erosion theory argue that it does not consider other factors that could have led to the erosion, such as the type of rock and the impact of local climate. Furthermore, they point out that there is no archaeological evidence to support the existence of a civilization in Egypt that could have built the Sphinx as early as 5000–7000 BCE.

While the water erosion theory is compelling to some, it remains a fringe theory and has not significantly altered the consensus on the Sphinx's age.

The exact date of the construction of the Great Sphinx of [[Giza]] in [[Egypt]] is still a subject of debate among historians and archaeologists. However, the most widely accepted theory is that it was built during the reign of [[Pharaoh Khafre (also known as Chefren)]], who ruled during the [[Fourth Dynasty]] of the [[Old Kingdom]], around [[2558–2532 BCE]]. The Great Sphinx is believed to have been carved from a single massive [[limestone]] outcrop, with the face of the Sphinx likely representing the pharaoh himself.

It is important to note that some alternative theories propose different construction dates, ranging from pre-dynastic times to the period of the Middle Kingdom. However, the consensus among scholars is that the Sphinx was most likely built during the reign of Khafre.
