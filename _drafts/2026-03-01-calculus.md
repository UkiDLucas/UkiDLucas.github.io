Calculus is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of rates of change and the accumulation of small changes to determine larger ones. It is a fundamental tool in fields such as physics, engineering, economics, and many others.

There are two main branches of calculus: differential calculus and integral calculus. Differential calculus deals with the study of rates of change and slopes of curves, while integral calculus deals with the accumulation of small changes to determine larger ones.

Some of the key concepts in calculus include limits, derivatives, integrals, and differential equations. Limits are used to define the behavior of functions as they approach certain values. Derivatives are used to find the rate at which a function is changing at a given point. Integrals are used to find the total accumulated change of a function over a certain interval. Differential equations are equations that involve derivatives and are used to model physical and natural phenomena.

Calculus has many applications in real-world situations, such as determining the velocity and acceleration of an object, finding optimal solutions to problems, and modeling natural phenomena such as population growth and chemical reactions.
