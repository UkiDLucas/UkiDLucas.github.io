The Post-Transformer Era Begins: Hyena Edge and the Rise of Private, On-Device AI

# Executive Summary

Liquid AI, a Boston-based startup born from MIT, has introduced Hyena Edge—a revolutionary AI model that operates entirely on smartphones like the Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra without relying on transformer-based attention mechanisms. Instead, it uses convolutional Hyena-Y operators, dramatically reducing latency, memory use, and power consumption while improving accuracy. Developed through an automated architecture search system called STAR, Hyena Edge represents a major leap forward in edge computing, enabling faster, more efficient, and privacy-preserving AI. Benchmarks reveal that Hyena Edge not only outperforms transformer counterparts in speed and memory efficiency but also delivers superior results across a range of language model benchmarks. Liquid AI plans to open-source this model, paving the way for a flourishing ecosystem of high-performance, on-device AI applications.

# Blog Chapters

## Chapter 1: Companies like Liquid AI support my intuition that TLM is the way of the future

When I first began exploring the limits of transformer-based AI, I suspected they weren't the endgame. Too power-hungry, too dependent on the cloud. Liquid AI’s Hyena Edge confirms what I’ve believed for a while now: we’re entering an era where Tiny Language Models, run locally, are more sustainable and more private.

The fact that Hyena Edge runs faster and more accurately than Transformer++ models—on a phone—feels like a validation of the edge-first approach I've been advocating. And if it fits between Spotify and your camera roll without draining your battery? That’s magic.

## Chapter 2: Let’s review STAR framework architecture

One of the more thrilling aspects of Hyena Edge is that it wasn’t handcrafted by a wizard engineer. It was evolved.

Liquid AI used STAR—Synthesis of Tailored Architectures—a sort of automated Darwinian lab. They started with 16 models, ran them across 24 generations, measuring latency and memory _on an actual phone_, and tracking accuracy on 5 billion-token test sets.

STAR combined convolution options (Hyena Full, X, Y) and attention variants, and then visualized each generation. Watching those histograms shift felt like natural selection in code. The winning survivor? A lean model with 32 layers, gated convolutions, and no hand-tuning.

## Chapter 3: Low power, high performance, and real-world honesty

Here’s the kicker: Hyena Edge doesn’t just outperform; it does so on real hardware. No theoretical benchmarks. No fudged numbers. The Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra was the testbed.

Short prompts, long prompts—it crushed them all. Latency was 30% lower in longer sequences, and even snappy with just 20-token requests, which matters for assistants, messages, and mobile input.

Even the accuracy improved. Wikitext, Lambada, ARC Challenge—Hyena Edge beat the baseline Transformer each time. That’s compelling if you want speed _and_ substance.

## Chapter 4: Open sourcing Hyena Edge is an invitation to all of us

What gets me excited most isn’t just the benchmarks—it’s that Liquid AI is sharing this. They’re open-sourcing Hyena Edge. That means it’ll run on more than just fancy phones: think Raspberry Pi, iPads, watchOS.

This is our chance to fork, fine-tune, port, and build on top of something lean and brilliant. Privacy-preserving AI, without cloud dependencies, is becoming real.

## Chapter 5: What this means for my work and yours

In AIKO, I’m building a system that helps people connect with their notes, ideas, and emotions—all without needing the internet. Hyena Edge, or something like it, could become part of that brain.

I want agents that don’t snoop, that can run offline, that feel personal. Liquid AI just handed us a blueprint for how that future might be built. Let’s explore what we can do with it.
