The previous version was a widely accepted English translation but still modernized. Here’s a more literal translation closely following the original Hebrew meaning and wording:

1. I am YHWH your God, who brought you out from the land of Egypt, from the house of slavery. You shall have no other gods beside me.

2. You shall not make for yourself a carved image, nor any likeness of what is in the heavens above or on the earth below or in the waters beneath the earth. You shall not bow down to them or serve them; for I, YHWH your God, am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children to the third and fourth generation of those who hate me, but showing steadfast love to thousands of those who love me and keep my commandments.

3. You shall not lift up the name of YHWH your God in vain; for YHWH will not hold guiltless anyone who lifts up His name in vain.

4. Remember the Sabbath day to sanctify it. Six days you shall labor and do all your work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath to YHWH your God. You shall not do any work—you, nor your son, nor your daughter, nor your male servant, nor your female servant, nor your livestock, nor the sojourner within your gates. For in six days YHWH made the heavens and the earth, the sea and all that is in them, but rested on the seventh day. Therefore YHWH blessed the Sabbath day and made it holy.

5. Honor your father and your mother, so your days may be prolonged upon the land that YHWH your God is giving to you.

6. You shall not murder.

7. You shall not commit adultery.

8. You shall not steal.

9. You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor.

10. You shall not covet your neighbor’s house. You shall not covet your neighbor’s wife, nor his male servant, nor his female servant, nor his ox, nor his donkey, nor anything that belongs to your neighbor.

  

This version emphasizes direct translation of original Hebrew phrasing, names (“YHWH” instead of “the LORD”), and more explicitly preserves the original cultural and religious context.



Original Deities (Sky Fathers):

• Elohim (Canaanite/Hebrew tradition) – A generalized divine concept, preceding the personal deity YHWH.

• Dyaus Pitar (Vedic tradition) – The original Sky Father deity in early Vedic texts.

• Rod or Svarog (Slavic tradition): Considered the primordial, ancestral god from whom other deities descend or evolve.

• Thunder Avatars (Warrior/Storm manifestations):

• YHWH (Hebrew)—originally a storm/warrior deity becoming universalized.

• Indra (Vedic)—storm/warrior deity who took primacy in Rigvedic mythology.

• Jupiter/Zeus (Roman/Greek)—storm-ruling gods with lightning powers, often depicted as active protectors.

• Perun/Pierun (Slavic) and Thor (Norse)—strong thunder gods associated with protection and order, each emerging from earlier sky-deity traditions.

  

Conceptually summarized:

• The older sky gods (like Elohim, Dyaus, Rod) represent universal cosmic order, authority, and creation—more passive and overarching.

• The later storm or thunder “avatars” (YHWH, Jupiter, Indra, Perun, Thor) are active, involved gods who enforce justice, protection, and societal order through direct interaction (storms, lightning, warfare).
