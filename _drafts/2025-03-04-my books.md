 #byUkiDLucas #public
 
 
- **"Ariowie Slowianie Polacy" (Polish Edition) by Mariusz Kowalski**  
    Explores the historical and cultural roots of the Slavic peoples—particularly the Poles—by examining ancient chronicles, linguistic evidence, and archaeological findings.
    
- **"Atlas Shrugged" by Ayn Rand**  
    A philosophical novel that combines elements of science fiction and mystery, focusing on Rand’s objectivist philosophy and the role of individual achievement in society.
    
- **"Escaping the Build Trap" by Melissa Perri**  
    Guides product managers and organizations on how to shift from simply shipping features to truly discovering and delivering value, with practical frameworks and real-world examples.
    
- **"How to Win Friends and Influence People" by Dale Carnegie**  
    A timeless self-help classic offering strategies for improving interpersonal relationships, communication skills, and leadership effectiveness.
    
- **"Making Children Mind without Losing Yours" by Kevin Leman**  
    A parenting guide that provides principles and tactics to encourage positive child behavior, emphasizing mutual respect, consistent discipline, and healthy boundaries.
    
- **"Molecules: The Elements and the Architecture of Everything" by Theodore Gray and Nick Mann**  
    A visually striking exploration of how atoms bond into molecules, complete with photographs and explanations that illuminate the building blocks of matter.
    
- **"Plant Variation and Evolution" by David Briggs and S. M. Walters**  
    Examines how plants adapt and evolve over time, discussing genetic variation, environmental pressures, and the implications for biodiversity and conservation.
    
- **"ProBlogger" by Darren Rowse and Chris Garrett**  
    Practical advice on how to start and grow a blog, covering everything from setting up a website to monetization strategies, branding, and audience engagement.
    
- **"Rodowód Słowian" (Genealogy of the Slavs) by Tomasz Kosiński**  
    Delves into the origins of Slavic peoples, presenting theories, historical records, and linguistic research on early Slavic migrations and settlements.
    
- **"Słowiańscy Królowie" (Slavic Kings) by Janusz Bieszki**  
    Focuses on the legends and histories of early Slavic rulers, aiming to shed light on their roles and influence in shaping the region’s medieval society.
    
- **"STARA BAŚŃ" by Józef Ignacy Kraszewski**  
    A historical novel set in the pre-Polish Piast era, weaving together myth and legend to depict the customs, beliefs, and struggles of early Slavic tribes.
    
- **"Storyworthy: Engage, Teach, Persuade, and Change Your Life through the Power of Storytelling" by Mathew Dicks**  
    Offers techniques for crafting and delivering compelling personal stories, underlining their power in communication, teaching, persuasion, and self-discovery.
    
- **"Superfoods - The Food and Medicine of the Future" by David Wolfe**  
    Explores various nutrient-rich foods, explaining their health benefits and suggesting ways to incorporate them into daily diets for optimal well-being.
    
- **"Tamata and the Alliance" by Bernard Moitessier**  
    A sailing memoir recounting Moitessier’s extraordinary solo voyages, reflections on maritime life, and the spiritual aspects of long-distance ocean travel.
    
- **"The Age of Spiritual Machines: When Computers Exceed Human Intelligence" by Ray Kurzweil**  
    Predicts the evolution of computing technology and artificial intelligence, discussing how rapid advancements could shape society, consciousness, and human potential.
    
- **"The Black Monk" by Anton Chekhov**  
    A short story that examines madness, creativity, and the complexity of human psychology through the experiences of a scholar haunted by a mysterious monk.
    
- **"The Cricket on the Hearth: A Fairy Tale of Home" by Charles Dickens**  
    A heartwarming Christmas novella focusing on themes of family, kindness, and domestic harmony, symbolized by the cheerful cricket’s presence in a humble home.
    
- **"The Maya" by Michael D. Coe**  
    A comprehensive overview of the ancient Maya civilization—its history, society, art, and writing system—featuring key archaeological findings and scholarly insights.
    
- **"Various Books" by Charles Dickens**  
    Refers to Dickens’s broader body of work, renowned for vivid characters, social commentary, and memorable tales such as "Oliver Twist," "Great Expectations," and "A Christmas Carol."
    
- **"Wandalowie czyli Polacy" (Polish Edition) by Pawel Szydlowski**  
    Argues a controversial standpoint connecting the Vandals with early Polish history, offering genealogical and linguistic evidence to support this theory.
