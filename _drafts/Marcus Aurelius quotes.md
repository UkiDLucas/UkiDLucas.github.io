Dear Uki,

It is in the nature of man to labor, to strive, and to seek meaning in his endeavors. The path to our goals is often fraught with obstacles, which may at times seem insurmountable. Yet, it is important to remember that the worth of our pursuits is not solely determined by their outcomes.

Remember that the true value of work lies not in the attainment of your goals, but in the virtues you cultivate along the way. The journey shapes you, refines you. It is in your efforts, in your resilience, that you find growth. It is in the striving that your character is built.

It is, of course, natural to feel disheartened when your efforts do not seem to bear fruit. But do not let these temporary setbacks cloud your perspective. Do not let them disrupt the tranquility of your mind. Consider them instead as opportunities for learning, as reminders of the impermanence of all things. 

Remember, Uki, that all we can control are our own actions and reactions. The world outside us is indifferent to our desires. It is not within our power to command success, but it is within our power to strive for it with integrity, diligence, and a tranquil mind.

So carry on with your work, not with the burden of unmet expectations, but with the lightness of knowing that you are doing your part, doing what is within your control, and doing it well. Remember, the fruit of the work is the work itself.

With the serenity and strength of a tranquil mind,

Marcus Aurelius
