I have been reviewing the [State of AI 2023](https://www.stateof.ai/), and inspired by it, I wanted to share some of my thoughts.
There is no question that the use of text, images, and sound, also called "multi-modality" in Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, is the topic of the day.

However, as someone working with 3D perception sensors, I believe the true revolution will happen when Machine Learning incorporates spatial thinking.
We already have excellent 3D modeling in games and simulations. Still, the actual floodgate opening will come with fleets of vehicles adding 3D sensors such as LiDARs.
With massive datasets collected on the roads, there will be an industry similar to image object recognition, which started in 2012.
There will be a need to take the point cloud data produced by LiDARS and abstract it into a mesh of polygons similar to that used in games. 
The shapes of various objects, even if partially obstructed, will need to be classified into thousands of categories even when rotated at any angle. 
Abstraction might be the key. Recognizing primitives, such as wheels, doors, and windows, might give us a graph of relationships indicating the closest relationship to an automobile, adding a horse to a mix might give us a buggy, and so on.
