# How to reset the BIOS on HP Z620?

Resetting the BIOS on an [[HP Z620 Workstation]] involves clearing the [[CMOS]] memory. Here's how you can do it:

Important: Clearing the CMOS will reset the BIOS settings to their default values. Before proceeding, ensure you have noted down any custom settings you may have configured in the BIOS.

1. Power off your HP Z620 workstation and disconnect it from the power source.
    
2. Locate the [[CMOS battery]] on the [[motherboard]]. The CMOS battery is a small coin-shaped cell typically found on the motherboard. It may be secured in a holder or clipped onto the board.
    
3. Carefully remove the CMOS battery from its slot. You can use a small flat-head screwdriver or your fingers to gently pop it out.
    
4. After removing the battery, wait for at least 5-10 minutes to ensure that the CMOS memory completely loses power and resets.
    
5. While waiting, you can also press and hold the power button on the workstation for about 15 seconds to discharge any residual power.
    
6. After the waiting period, reinsert the CMOS battery back into its slot. Ensure that it is properly seated in the holder or clip.
    
7. Reconnect the power cable to the workstation.
    
8. Power on the workstation and access the BIOS settings. To do this, press the appropriate key repeatedly during the boot process. For HP workstations like the Z620, the usual key is either "[[F10 key]]" or "[[F1 key]]," but it may vary depending on the specific model. Look for an on-screen prompt that indicates which key to press for BIOS setup.
    
9. Once you are in the BIOS settings, you may need to set the date, time, and any other custom settings according to your preferences.
    
10. Save the changes and exit the BIOS. Typically, you can do this by pressing the "F10" key to save and exit.
    

Your BIOS settings should now be reset to their default values. If there were any issues related to BIOS settings causing boot problems, they should now be resolved. However, keep in mind that this process will not fix hardware issues; it is primarily for resetting the BIOS configuration. If you continue to experience problems with your HP Z620, it's recommended to seek further assistance from HP support or a qualified technician.
