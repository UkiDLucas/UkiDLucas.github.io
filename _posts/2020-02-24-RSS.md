We all have our own ways to keep track of the good blog posts we have read, some post them on their social media, some bookmark them, and some use apps like Google Keep. A good solution for keeping in touch with your favorite bloggers is a RSS Reader Chrome extension.

RSS means "really simple syndication", or making the blog available programatically for other tools to consume it. This technology is decades old, I thought a quick refresher on how to use it might be needed.



## Install Chrome Extension for RSS Reader



There are many RSS extensions you choose from, I picked [one](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/rss-feed-reader/pnjaodmkngahhkoihejjehlcdlnohgmp/related) that has 4.5 starts and over 7,000 users.

This extension in it's free version offers feed updates every 2 hours which is fine with me.



![Add_RSS_extension](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/UkiDLucas/UkiDLucas.github.io/master/_posts/2020-02-24-RSS-reader/Add_RSS_extension.png)





## Locating the RSS feed on the blog

The web sites that you can subscribe to will have the following icon. Once you have the RSS reader installed, just click on it and it should subscribe.

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/UkiDLucas/UkiDLucas.github.io/master/_posts/2020-02-24-RSS-reader/RSS_subscribe.png)



The subscription process may vary, but this is what you might expect.

![Adding the RSS site](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/UkiDLucas/UkiDLucas.github.io/master/_posts/2020-02-24-RSS-reader/Adding_RSS_site.png)







## Accessign RSS Reader in Chrome browser menu

Once all is set up you can receive updates and read articles by clicking the orange RSS reader icon.



![RSS extension menu](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/UkiDLucas/UkiDLucas.github.io/master/_posts/2020-02-24-RSS-reader/RSS_extension_menu.png)
