The trend of counter-signaling and race to the bottom is worrying me.

For the people who are confused by the title, let me explain. 

Flexing, as in muscle-flexing of the bodybuilder, is used when a young millionaire jumps out of his new Lamborghini Egoista on the Sunset Boulevard, Beverly Hills. 

Walter de Silva, the designer, said that the Egoista "represents hedonism taken to the extreme". Precisely!

OK, I know it is a concept car, but it was a perfect name for this illustration.

The extension of the same concept, is signaling. The term is used in biology. Peacocks and other birds with their oversized plume send the message, "look I am so healthy that I can afford extravagance" even if it kills me in the end. Another example of signaling is the antler on a stag, but at least antler can be used as defensive weapon.

Most of (more sophisticated) people have a distaste of excessive flexing and signaling in people. We still think birds and stags are wonderful.

I leave the above part to your own judgement without saying more.

The trend of counter-signaling is the opposite. 

In (human and) animal word the individual that is walking calmly in face of danger is mostly left alone. This strategy has probably saved my hide in the worse of Chicago neighborhoods one or twice.

The new extreme-counter-signaling trend however got to be stupid.

Let me give you a couple examples.

"I am a millionaire startup founder or executive and I drive my old, bit up car." 

Stupid! I drove old cars too much in my life. They break. On my Astro van a wheel literally fell off on the highway. On my Chevy Cavalier the roof leaked. I donated both of the cars to more needy people, each one of the ended in the junk yard soon after. 
The old cars are unreliable and unsafe for your kids. People who drive old cars do not show to work on time and miss important meetings with the clients. It is bad practice and I vowed to use a recent model car as soon as I could afford them.

"I am a leader and I stop to pick up a penny of the pavement."

In the most extreme movie example Fb founder shows up to the venture capital interview unwashed and in a bath robe just to flex how much he does not care about them.

The race to the bottom.

In the great book "Storytelling" the author says that story tellers and comedians use the tactic of putting themselves down. Being vonureable. Please note it is a tactic, a trick.

Unfortunately, this turned in recent times into a race to the bottom.

A guy wears an ugly $2 Casio watch from the 1980s to signal God knows what while speaking from his island villa in Dubai. I lived in 1980s, these watches were not cool then and they are still not cool. He probably spent $300 to find it, anyway.
