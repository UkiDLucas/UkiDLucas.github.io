Books printed with Ancient or Koine Greek originals are typically editions of classical literature, religious texts, and scholarly works. Here are some key categories and examples:

### Classical Literature:
1. **Homer**:
   - "The Iliad" (Ἰλιάς)
   - "The Odyssey" (Ὀδύσσεια)
2. **Sophocles**:
   - "Oedipus Rex" (Οἰδίπους Τύραννος)
   - http://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/17839
   - "Antigone" (Ἀντιγόνη)
3. **Euripides**:
   - "Medea" (Μήδεια)
   - "The Bacchae" (Βάκχαι)
4. **Aeschylus**:
   - "The Oresteia" (Ὀρέστεια)
5. **Aristophanes**:
   - "The Clouds" (Νεφέλαι)
   - "Lysistrata" (Λυσιστράτη)
6. **Herodotus**:
   - "Histories" (Ἱστορίαι)
7. **Thucydides**:
   - "History of the Peloponnesian War" (Ἱστορίαι)

### Philosophical Works:
1. **Plato**:
   - "The Republic" (Πολιτεία)
   - "The Symposium" (Συμπόσιον)
2. **Aristotle**:
   - "Nicomachean Ethics" (Ἠθικὰ Νικομάχεια)
   - "Politics" (Πολιτικά)

### Religious Texts:
1. **The New Testament**:
   - The entire New Testament, originally written in Koine Greek, is available in various editions. Examples include:
     - The Nestle-Aland Novum Testamentum Graece
     - The United Bible Societies' Greek New Testament
2. **The Septuagint (LXX)**:
   - The Greek translation of the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament), widely used in scholarly and religious studies.
3. **Patristic Writings**:
   - Works by early Church Fathers, such as:
     - "Confessions" by Augustine of Hippo (in Greek translation)
     - "Against Heresies" (Adversus Haereses) by Irenaeus

### Scholarly Editions:
1. **The Loeb Classical Library**:
   - This series publishes Greek and Latin texts with facing English translations, covering a wide range of classical authors.
2. **Oxford Classical Texts (OCT)**:
   - This series provides critical editions of Greek and Latin works with scholarly notes.
3. **Bibliotheca Teubneriana**:
   - Another series offering critical editions of Greek and Latin texts, often used in academic settings.

### Miscellaneous:
1. **Papyri Editions**:
   - Collections of ancient Greek papyri, such as the Oxyrhynchus Papyri, often published by institutions like the Egypt Exploration Society.
2. **Inscriptions**:
   - Publications of ancient Greek inscriptions, such as the "Corpus Inscriptionum Graecarum" (CIG) and "Inscriptiones Graecae" (IG).

These books are valuable resources for students, scholars, and enthusiasts of Ancient and Koine Greek, offering insights into the language, literature, philosophy, and history of ancient Greece and the early Christian era.
