Historically, humans have thrived because they are useful to one another. Communities naturally ostracized (expelled) malicious or unproductive people, effectively making usefulness synonymous with survival. Being excluded was not merely inconvenient; it often meant death, as individuals rarely survived in isolation.

This ancient principle persists today, albeit differently; people who fail to contribute positively or display harmful behaviors are typically ousted from modern communities, such as workplaces. The outcome is not as dire as in the past, yet it can still significantly impact an individual's livelihood and well-being.

Looking forward, our adaptability as humans remains one of our greatest strengths. With the rapid rise of artificial intelligence, adaptability will be key to future survival and success.

Rather than viewing AI as a threat, humans must find new ways to be useful by collaborating with AI systems. In this partnership, humans can leverage creativity, empathy, and strategic vision to complement AI's computational strengths.

In short, as we transition into an AI-driven future, remaining adaptable and finding ways to meaningfully collaborate with AI will define our continued relevance and prosperity.
