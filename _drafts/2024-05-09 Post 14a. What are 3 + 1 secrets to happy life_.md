This morning, my daughter was a little overwhelmed; I could see she was about to cry.

We sat down on the staircase, and I cuddled her, I told her that there are 3 secrets to being happy:

1. See the world for what it is. Often, life is hard, and kids on the bus are mean. Accept it; flow around rocks as water flows in a stream.

2. Do your best at a given moment. Do not worry about the past or the future; just do your best now and be happy about it. Doing best may include planning, dreaming, and creating something new. Not just tasks and duties.

3. Do not worry too much. Do not care too much what people think about the outcomes of your best efforts as long as you know you did your best at the given moment.

4. I told her that I do love her so much and hugged her for a long while.



Yup, there are really four things. Having a support network of people who love you is equally important, along with your own virtues and coping mechanisms.



I am not doing very well about #1.



Today, I felt guilty rushing to work to be on time, and I felt bad leaving home too early.
