In our interconnected world, some brilliant thinkers have emerged as influential internet writers, sharing their insights on technology, personal development, philosophy, and more. If you appreciate the works of Paul Graham, Tim Ferriss, and Gurwinder Bhogal, you'll likely enjoy the following thinkers as well:

1. **Naval Ravikant** - Co-founder of AngelList, Naval shares profound thoughts on wealth, happiness, and the future of technology through his tweets, podcasts, and blog posts. His insights often blend philosophy with practical advice.
   https://nav.al/subscribe

2. **Maria Popova** - Creator of Brain Pickings, Maria explores a wide range of topics from literature to science, weaving deep connections between them and offering rich, reflective commentary.
   https://www.themarginalian.org/

3. **Morgan Housel** - A partner at Collaborative Fund, Morgan writes about the psychology of money and investing. His blog, Collaborative Fund Blog, is known for its clear, insightful, and often counterintuitive takes on financial topics.
   https://collaborativefund.us10.list-manage.com/subscribe?u=8af03e2487d538f972a79316a&id=29993e6e8c

4. **Farnam Street (Shane Parrish)** - Shane’s blog, Farnam Street, focuses on mental models, decision making, and lifelong learning. It’s a treasure trove for those looking to sharpen their thinking and understand complex concepts.
   https://fs.blog/start/

5. **Scott Alexander** - Known for his blog Slate Star Codex (now Astral Codex Ten), Scott writes long-form essays that delve into topics like rationality, medicine, and philosophy with a unique blend of humor and rigorous analysis.
   https://substack.com/@astralcodexten
   https://www.astralcodexten.com/

6. **Wait But Why (Tim Urban)** - Tim’s blog, Wait But Why, tackles big ideas and complex topics like artificial intelligence, procrastination, and the future of humanity in a highly engaging and accessible way, often complemented by quirky illustrations.
   https://waitbutwhy.com/

7. **James Clear** - Author of "Atomic Habits," James writes extensively about habits, decision-making, and continuous improvement. His blog is filled with practical advice grounded in scientific research.
8. https://jamesclear.com/3-2-1

8. **Benedict Evans** - A technology analyst, Benedict shares thoughtful analyses on tech trends and their broader implications through his newsletter and blog, providing a nuanced perspective on the ever-evolving tech landscape.
9. https://www.ben-evans.com/newsletter

9. **Anne-Laure Le Cunff** - Founder of Ness Labs, Anne-Laure writes about mindful productivity, neuroscience, and learning, blending science and personal development in a thoughtful and accessible way.
   https://nesslabs.com/newsletter

10. **Mark Manson** - Best known for his book "The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck," Mark's blog covers personal development, relationships, and philosophy with a refreshingly candid and no-nonsense approach.
    https://markmanson.net/

These writers not only offer thought-provoking content but also provide practical insights that can inspire personal and professional growth. Dive into their works, and you'll find a wealth of knowledge that resonates with the ethos of modern thinking.

Certainly! Here are more contemporary thinkers and writers who share their insights on the internet:

11. **Seth Godin** - A marketing guru, Seth Godin’s daily blog covers everything from marketing and entrepreneurship to leadership and personal development. His concise and thought-provoking posts are a staple for many.
    https://seths.blog/subscribe/

12. **Austin Kleon** - Author of "Steal Like an Artist," Austin shares his thoughts on creativity and the artistic process through his blog and newsletter. His posts are often a mix of personal anecdotes, creative advice, and inspiration.
    https://austinkleon.com/newsletter/

13. **Kevin Kelly** - Co-founder of Wired magazine, Kevin writes extensively about technology, culture, and the future. His blog and book "The Inevitable" offer fascinating insights into the technological trends shaping our world.

14. **Ryan Holiday** - Known for popularizing Stoicism through his books like "The Daily Stoic" and "The Obstacle Is the Way," Ryan's blog covers philosophy, strategy, and self-improvement with a modern twist.

15. **Eric Weinstein** - A mathematician and economist, Eric shares his thoughts on a wide range of topics including science, economics, and culture. His podcast, "The Portal," dives deep into conversations with other leading thinkers.

16. **Ezra Klein** - Co-founder of Vox and a columnist for The New York Times, Ezra Klein writes and speaks about politics, policy, and social issues. His podcast, "The Ezra Klein Show," features in-depth interviews with experts across various fields.

17. **David Perell** - Known for his writing on the intersection of technology, culture, and writing, David’s blog and newsletter offer insights on how to improve your thinking and writing in the digital age.

18. **Adam Grant** - An organizational psychologist and author, Adam shares his research and insights on work, motivation, and productivity. His blog, books, and podcast "WorkLife" are highly regarded for their evidence-based approach.

19. **Maria Konnikova** - A psychologist and author, Maria writes about psychology, decision making, and human behavior. Her blog posts and books like "The Biggest Bluff" are engaging and deeply informative.

20. **Ali Abdaal** - A doctor turned productivity expert, Ali shares tips on productivity, learning, and life optimization through his blog, YouTube channel, and newsletter. His content is practical and often backed by scientific research.

21. **Andrew Huberman** - A neuroscientist and professor at Stanford University, Andrew shares his research and insights on brain function, performance, and health through his blog and the "Huberman Lab" podcast.

22. **Tim O'Reilly** - Founder of O'Reilly Media, Tim writes about the impact of technology on society, the economy, and the future of work. His blog offers deep dives into how technology can shape a better future.

23. **Annie Duke** - A former professional poker player turned decision strategist, Annie writes about decision-making, risk, and uncertainty. Her blog and books like "Thinking in Bets" provide valuable insights for making better choices.

24. **Julie Zhuo** - Former VP of Product Design at Facebook, Julie shares her thoughts on leadership, design, and personal growth through her blog and book "The Making of a Manager."

25. **Cal Newport** - An author and professor of computer science, Cal writes about deep work, digital minimalism, and productivity. His blog and books provide practical advice on how to focus and achieve meaningful work in a distracted world.

Exploring the writings of these thinkers will provide a rich and diverse perspective on a variety of topics relevant to both personal and professional growth. Happy reading!
