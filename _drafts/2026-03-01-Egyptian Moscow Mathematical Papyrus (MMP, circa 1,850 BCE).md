The Moscow Mathematical Papyrus (MMP), also known as the **Golenischev** Mathematical Papyrus [1], is an ancient Egyptian mathematical text, dating back to the [[Egyptian 11th dynasty]] or [[Egyptian 13th dynasty 1]], around 1,850 BCE. 

## Current location
The Moscow Mathematical Papyrus is named after its current location, the [[Pushkin State Museum of Fine Arts in Moscow, Russia]], where it has been since the early 20th century.

## Language
Like other Egyptian mathematical texts, the Moscow Mathematical Papyrus uses a system of [[hieratic script 1]], which is a simplified version of hieroglyphics used for writing on papyrus. The text is written in a **mixture of prose and mathematical notation**, providing both the problem statements and their solutions.

## Physical description

The papyrus is approximately **5.5 meters (18 feet) long** and consists of **25 mathematical problems, including practical problems related to daily life, as well as more abstract mathematical problems.** The problems involve [[arithmetic 1]], [[Algebra 1]], and [[geometry 1]]. 

## Mathematical problems described
1. Calculating the [[surface area of a hemisphere 1]], 
2. determining the [[volume of a frustum 1]], 
3. solving [[linear equations 1]].
