Geometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of shapes, sizes, positions, and dimensions of objects in space. It is concerned with the properties of points, lines, angles, curves, surfaces, and solids, and their relationships and interactions with each other.

Geometry is used to describe and analyze physical objects and phenomena in the real world, as well as to develop abstract concepts and theories. It has many practical applications in fields such as architecture, engineering, physics, computer graphics, and art.

Geometry is divided into several subfields, including Euclidean geometry, analytic geometry, differential geometry, and topology. Each of these subfields focuses on specific aspects of geometry and has its own techniques and tools for solving problems and proving theorems.
